Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026. This policy covers the Screenshot to Calendar app and its backend service, and explains what personal data we process, why, for how long, and the rights you have under UK GDPR.

What we store

  • Your Google account identity: email address and Google account ID, obtained when you sign in with Google.
  • A Google Calendar refresh token, encrypted at rest (AES-256-GCM), used solely to create and update calendar events you ask for.
  • Images you share into the app and the event data extracted from them. These are stored encrypted at rest in AWS (eu-west-2, London); see "International transfers" below for where they are processed.
  • Corrections you make to extracted event data.

How images are processed

To turn a screenshot into an event, the image is sent to Anthropic's Claude API to classify it and extract the event details, and venue names may be sent to Google's Places API to resolve addresses. Images are used only to provide the service. Extraction is automated; low-confidence results are held for you to review and approve in the app before any calendar event is created.

Our lawful basis (UK GDPR Article 6)

  • Performance of a contract (Art. 6(1)(b)) for the core service: storing your images, extracting event details, and creating the calendar events you request.
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)) for the optional "Help improve extraction" use described below. You can withdraw it at any time in Settings.
  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)) for keeping the service secure and preventing abuse.

Improving the product (opt-in only)

If — and only if — you enable "Help improve extraction" in Settings, your corrections and the associated images may be added to our internal evaluation dataset to improve the extraction prompt. This is off by default, and the consent state is recorded per correction at the time you make it. Turning it off stops any future correction from being used.

International transfers

Your data is stored in the UK (AWS London, eu-west-2). However, when an image is processed it is transmitted to Anthropic in the United States, and venue lookups and calendar operations involve Google. These transfers outside the UK are covered by the UK International Data Transfer Agreement / Standard Contractual Clauses in our agreements with those providers.

Sub-processors

  • Anthropic (US) — image classification and event extraction.
  • Google — sign-in (OAuth), Calendar, and Places (venue → address).
  • Amazon Web Services (UK, eu-west-2) — hosting and storage.

How long we keep it

  • Your account, images, events, and corrections are kept for as long as your account exists. You can delete any capture, or your whole account, at any time (see "Your rights").
  • Operational AI-usage logs (token and cost records, no image content) are automatically deleted after 90 days.
  • Data exports you generate expire after 7 days.
  • Backups of our database are retained on a rolling basis for up to 35 days and then overwritten. Deleted data is not restored into the live service; if a backup is ever restored, your deletion is re-applied.

Automated processing

Event extraction is automated, but you remain in control: results you can act on are shown in the app, low-confidence extractions wait for your review and approval, and you can edit or delete any event. Because creating a calendar entry has no legal or similarly significant effect and you review the outcome, this is not solely-automated decision-making under Article 22.

Your rights (UK GDPR)

  • Access & portability: Settings → Export my data produces a machine-readable copy of everything stored about you, including links to your source images.
  • Erasure: Settings → Delete account permanently removes all images and records and revokes the app's access to your Google account.
  • Rectification: correct extracted details in the app at any time.
  • Objection, restriction, and withdrawal of consent: contact us, or toggle "Help improve extraction" off, at any time.
  • Complaint: you can lodge a complaint with the UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk.

What we never do

We do not sell data, run third-party ads or analytics, or read calendar events other than in the date window checked for duplicates in the calendar you selected.
