Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026. This policy covers the Screenshot to Calendar app and its backend service.

What we store

  • Your Google account identity: email address and Google account ID, obtained when you sign in with Google.
  • A Google Calendar refresh token, encrypted at rest (AES-256-GCM), used solely to create and update calendar events you ask for.
  • Images you share into the app and the event data extracted from them, stored encrypted at rest in AWS (eu-west-2, London).
  • Corrections you make to extracted event data.

How images are processed

Images are sent to Anthropic's Claude API to classify them and extract event details, and venue names may be sent to Google's Places API to resolve addresses. Images are used only to provide the service.

Improving the product (opt-in only)

If — and only if — you enable "Help improve extraction" in Settings, your corrections and the associated images may be added to our internal evaluation dataset to improve the extraction prompt. This is off by default, and the consent state is recorded per correction at the time you make it.

Your rights (GDPR)

  • Export: Settings → Export my data produces a complete copy of everything stored about you.
  • Deletion: Settings → Delete account permanently removes all images and records and revokes the app's access to your Google account.

What we never do

We do not sell data, run third-party ads or analytics, or read calendar events other than in the date window checked for duplicates in the calendar you selected.

Contact

Data controller: the operator of this app instance. Contact details are provided inside the app's Settings screen.
